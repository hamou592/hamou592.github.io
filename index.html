The Founder Of The Algerian Secrets Services
ABDELHAFID BOUSSOUF

This website is a work submitted by the student Hamou Nasreddine about a special person who played an important role during the Algerian revolution

Abdelhafid boussouf was born in the city of Mila in the old Mila neighborhood, his poor family was practicing farming. At the age of about eight, he entered the French school, where he studied and received a primary certificate. He joined the ranks of the people's party in the city of Mila and founded cells in it that include a large group of activists of the city, including Lakhdar bin Tubal and Anan Derradji, he would meet with the activists at his house, which was a refuge for various revolutionary and political faces that exploded the revolution of 1954.

When he joined the Algerian people's party in Constantine, he got acquainted with Mohamed Boudiaf, Larbi Ben M'hidi, Ben Toubal and others,At the outbreak of the Algerian revolution, he was appointed deputy of Larbi Ben M'hidi in the fifth region of Oran, in charge of Tlemcen.In 1950, he turned to secret work, first in the city of Skikda, and then in the Oran region, where he was neither known to French interests nor pursued by him.

After the somam conference he became a member of the National Council of the Algerian revolution, appointed Minister of Public Communications and armaments in the interim government. He founded the Algerian Secrets Services in 1957 and played a big role in the formation of frames in this field until he was nicknamed the father of The Algerian Secrets Services ,He was able to collect 8 billion old French francs during the Algerian revolution thanks to his skill and wit, in exchange for his trade in international Secrets Services,where he sold information to the United States, the Soviet Union, China, Japan, and this information was related to international affairs of these countries of interest.

Abdelhafid established a powerful Secrets Services for the revolution and was able to recruit some ministers in the French government for the Algerian revolution, including Michel Dupre, who was prime minister in the government of Charles de Gaulle, Minister of economy Fourcade, Minister of Agriculture Edgar Pisani and other figures related to the government. The end

Abdelhafid boussouf died on December 31, 1980 in Paris, and the Algerian government allocated a private plane to transport his body to Algeria. In 2014, the University of Mela, his hometown, was named after him
